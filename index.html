Vision

 The fixed gear community dreams of a world where biking transcends mere transportation or recreation to become a way of life. It's a tight-knit group where cyclists celebrate the straightforwardness, liberty, and ingenuity of fixed gear riding. They're united by a love for simplicity, city exploration, and personal expression through bike customization. In this vision, inclusivity, friendship, and a shared affection for the open road define the fixed gear community, advocating for cycling as a lifestyle accessible to everyone.



Mission

  The fixed gear community's mission is to nurture a dynamic cycling culture that transcends the boundaries of mere transportation or athletics, embracing it as a lifestyle. Our goal is to cultivate a sense of solidarity among cyclists who value the simplicity, liberty, and innovation inherent in fixed gear biking. By sharing our enthusiasm for minimalism, urban discovery, and individualized bike modification, we endeavor to establish a welcoming and encouraging atmosphere. Our aim is to share the pleasures of cycling and advocate for it as an inclusive lifestyle option for individuals of all walks of life.



History

  Fixed gear bikes have been around since the early days of cycling, originally emerging in the late 19th century. They were the standard type of bicycle before freewheel mechanisms were invented. This means that whenever the wheels turned, the pedals turned too. Initially popularized in track cycling events due to their simplicity and efficiency, fixed gear bikes later found favor among urban cyclists and bicycle messengers for their low maintenance and maneuverability in city environments. In the late 20th and early 21st centuries, fixed gear bikes experienced a resurgence in popularity, becoming a subculture favorite for their minimalist style and customization options. They remain a staple in urban cycling communities today, loved for their direct connection to the road and the sense of freedom they provide.
